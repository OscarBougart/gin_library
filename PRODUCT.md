# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults in and around Aachen looking for an evening out: couples, friends, and small groups seeking an intimate, upmarket bar; plus companies sourcing a venue for corporate events and gin tastings. They arrive on the marketing site (often on a phone, often in the evening) to gauge the mood, confirm it's the kind of place they want, find the address and hours, and reserve. German speakers are the default audience; English is a first-class second language. The job to be done: "decide this is worth the trip, then get in touch."

## Product Purpose

A bilingual one-page marketing site for The Gin Library, a hidden speakeasy gin and cocktail bar at Am Büchel 11 in Aachen. It sells the atmosphere (discreet, candlelit, library-quiet, gin-obsessed), showcases the cocktails and the room through photography, presents two bookable experiences (corporate events, €69 gin tastings), and routes visitors to reserve by phone or email. Success is a visitor who feels the mood through the screen and contacts the bar.

## Brand Personality

Intimate, refined, and quietly mysterious. The voice is hospitable but unhurried, confident without shouting: "find the entrance and you step into a world where bar craft meets the quiet of a library." Personality in three words: discreet, crafted, warm-dark. Emotional goal is anticipation and trust, the sense of being let in on a secret worth keeping. Dark room, gold light, condensed-caps display type, real photography.

## Anti-references

- **Loud nightclub / neon.** No high-saturation party energy, club-flyer layouts, or EDM aesthetics. The mood is candlelit and hushed, not strobe-lit.
- **Corporate SaaS clean.** No Inter/geometric-sans body, blue accents, flat icon-card grids, or startup-landing minimalism. This is hospitality, not a product page.
- Also avoid the generic Squarespace-restaurant template (stock hero + identical cards + default serif) and rustic-farmhouse warmth (cream paper, hand-drawn, cozy-cafe). Warmth here is gold-on-near-black, not kraft-and-linen.

## Design Principles

- **Let the room speak.** Photography of the bar and the drinks carries the mood; type and color frame it rather than compete with it.
- **Discretion over volume.** Restraint is the brand. One deliberate section kicker, not an eyebrow on every heading; gold used as a highlight, not a flood.
- **Bilingual as a first-class citizen.** German default, English equal; copy and layout must hold up in both languages without breaking.
- **Mood before information, then a clear way in.** The scroll builds atmosphere, but hours, address, and a reservation path are always reachable.
- **Craft you can feel.** Motion, spacing, and typography should reward attention the way a well-made cocktail does, never call attention to the mechanism.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**. Dark theme with light text and a bright gold accent; body and label contrast must stay ≥4.5:1 (large/bold ≥3:1). Visible keyboard focus on every interactive element. Animation-heavy by design, so `prefers-reduced-motion` must fully neutralize entrance, reveal, and sheen motion. Minimum 44×44px touch targets. Bilingual `<html lang>` kept in sync with the active language.
